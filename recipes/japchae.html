Japchae

Description

A tasty and fun Korean dish, Japchae's sesame flavour really shines through. The fresh vegetables don't hurt either.

Ingredients

  • 1 package sweet potato noodles (250g)e
  • 1 medium red pepper (200g)
  • 1 small carrot (130g)
  • 1 medium onion (150g)
  • 3 scallion stalks
  • 3 garlic cloves
  • large handful of spinach
  • toasted sesame seeds
  • 5 tbsp soy sauce
  • 2 tbsp sugar
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil and cook sweet potato noodles following the directions on the package. Once cooked, drain and rinse with cold water to stop the cooking process. Add 1 tsp of sesame oil to coat the noodles, and then use scissors to cut the noodles (around 3-4 times). In the same pot, add in the spinach and blanch for 30 seconds. Rinse with cold water and squeeze the excess liquid out using a towel. Chop the bundled up spinach in half.
  2. Slice all the vegetables into match stick sized pieces. They should all be relatively the same size.
  3. Put all the sauce ingredients into a small bowl and stir.
  4. In the same wok, fry onions and garlic for 1 minute. Then add in the carrot and fry for another minute. Add in the red pepper and scallions and fry for one more minute. Slightly turn down the heat and add in the noodles, spinach and sauce. Toss and cook until all the sauce is all soaked up.
  5. Serve and garnish with toasted sesame seeds.
